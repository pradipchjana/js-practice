# General Knowledge Guessing Game

Welcome to the five-clue challenge Your goal is to **guess the answer** with the fewest clues possible.

You have a maximum of **five clues**. If you guess correctly on the first clue you win big If you use all five clues and still can't guess you lose the round.

Good luck

---

## **Round 1 Target Landmark**

**The Answer is The Great Wall of China**

**Clue 1** My construction started over two thousand years ago

**Clue 2** I am often mistakenly said to be visible from space with the naked eye

**Clue 3** My purpose was to protect various Chinese dynasties

**Clue 4** My structure is not one continuous line but a series of walls and fortifications

**Clue 5** I am the longest man-made structure in the world

## **Round 2 Target Historical Figure**

**The Answer is Marie Curie**

**Clue 1** I was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize

**Clue 2** My groundbreaking work involved **radioactivity**

**Clue 3** I am the only person to win a Nobel Prize in **two different scientific fields** Physics and Chemistry

**Clue 4** I helped create mobile X-ray units used during World War I known as **petites Curies**

**Clue 5** My maiden name was Sklodowska

## **Round 3 Target Scientific Concept**

**The Answer is Photosynthesis**

**Clue 1** It is a process essential for nearly all life on Earth

**Clue 2** It primarily occurs in the **leaves** of plants

**Clue 3** The main products are **glucose and oxygen**

**Clue 4** It requires three main ingredients **water carbon dioxide and sunlight**

**Clue 5** The word literally means **putting together with light**

## **Round 5 Target Natural Phenomenon**

**The Answer is Aurora Borealis** or **Northern Lights**

**Clue 1** It is caused by **solar winds** interacting with the Earths magnetic field

**Clue 2** It is primarily seen near the **North Pole**

**Clue 3** The colors are caused by different gases such as **oxygen and nitrogen**

**Clue 4** Its name comes from the Roman goddess of dawn and the Greek word for north wind

**Clue 5** It can also be observed near the South Pole where it is called the **Southern Lights**
